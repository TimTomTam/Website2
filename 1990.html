The 90's

The 32-bit microprocessor dominated the consumer market in the 1990s. Processor clock speeds increased by more than tenfold between 1990 and 1999, and 64-bit processors began to emerge later in the decade. In the 1990s, microprocessors no longer used the same clock speed for the processor and the RAM. Processors began to have a front-side bus (FSB) clock speed used in communication with RAM and other components. Typically, the processor itself ran at a clock speed that was a multiple of the FSB clock speed.

The Intel Pentium, although not as powerful as its competitors, reached an impressive clock speed of 60-66 MHz (1993)
The third generation Pentium is 10 times more powerful than the first at 600 MHz (1999)
